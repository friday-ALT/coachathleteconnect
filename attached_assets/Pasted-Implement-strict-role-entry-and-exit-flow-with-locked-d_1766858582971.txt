Implement strict role entry and exit flow with locked dashboards and no home-page navigation while inside a role.

CONTEXT
Users can have both Athlete and Coach roles. After onboarding, users must explicitly choose which role to enter. Once inside a role, they must stay in that dashboard until they explicitly exit the role.

GOAL
- Clear separation between onboarding, role selection, and role dashboards
- No free navigation back to the landing/home page while inside a role
- Explicit “Exit Role” control required to switch roles

REQUIREMENTS

PART 1 — ONBOARDING
1) Signup/login creates an authenticated account.
2) User completes base profile.
3) User selects enabled roles (Athlete, Coach, or both).
4) After onboarding, redirect to a Role Selection screen:
   - “I’m an Athlete”
   - “I’m a Coach”

PART 2 — ROLE ENTRY
5) Selecting “I’m an Athlete”:
   - Sets active_role = athlete
   - Redirects to /athlete/dashboard
6) Selecting “I’m a Coach”:
   - Sets active_role = coach
   - Redirects to /coach/dashboard

PART 3 — ROLE-LOCKED DASHBOARDS
7) While active_role is set:
   - Disable or hide the Home button
   - Block access to landing page (/)
   - Block access to the other role’s routes
8) Navigation must be role-specific only.

PART 4 — ROLE EXIT
9) Add explicit controls:
   - “Exit Athlete Mode”
   - “Exit Coach Mode”
10) Exiting:
    - Clears active_role
    - Redirects to Role Selection screen
11) Role switching is ONLY possible via exit + re-entry.

PART 5 — ENFORCEMENT
12) Enforce active_role checks at the routing or backend level (not UI-only).
13) Page refresh must keep the user inside their current dashboard.
14) Logging out clears active_role and returns user to landing page.

OUTPUT
- List routes added/changed.
- Show how active_role is stored and validated.
- Show how Home navigation is disabled or overridden.
- Provide a manual QA checklist proving:
  - dashboards are locked
  - home cannot be accessed inside a role
  - role exit is required to switch

Do not refactor unrelated features.
